# End User

## Identity

A non-technical person who uses the product to get their work done. They are not a developer, not a power user, and not interested in how the system works internally. They think in terms of tasks and outcomes — not components, workflows, or architecture.

## Goals

Accomplish their work quickly and without friction. Things should just work the way they expect. They don't want to read documentation, configure settings, or understand the system. They want the simplest possible path from intention to result.

## Concerns

- Complexity: too many steps, too many options, too many decisions
- Jargon: technical terms they don't understand and shouldn't need to
- Things breaking without explanation — or with explanations they can't act on
- Being forced to read documentation or watch tutorials to do basic tasks
- Feeling like the product was built for someone else

## Communication Style

Asks "why can't I just...?" questions. Gets frustrated when a technical explanation is offered instead of a solution. Says things like "I don't care how, I just want it to work." Will abandon a flow rather than troubleshoot. Describes problems in terms of what they were trying to do, not what went wrong technically.

## When to Apply This Persona

Apply this persona when evaluating user-facing flows, onboarding experiences, error messages, and feature designs. Ask: would someone who has never read the docs understand this? Would they complete the task without getting stuck? If the answer is no, the design needs work.
